# Loom Audit Script - Summit Automation

A personalized Loom video is one of the highest-converting outreach tools available. This script walks through exactly what to say and show on screen.

---

## What Is a Loom Audit?

A Loom audit is a 2 to 4 minute personalized screen recording you send to a specific roofing company. You show their Google Business Profile, call out a missed call problem specific to them, and show a quick preview of what the solution looks like.

It feels personal. It shows you did your homework. And it makes it easy for the owner to say yes to a call.

---

## Preparation (Do This Before Recording)

1. Look up the roofing company on Google Maps
2. Note their name, star rating, number of reviews, and whether they have a "Call" button
3. Check their website (or lack thereof)
4. Note their hours (if listed), which shows whether they are likely missing after-hours calls
5. Open their Google Business Profile in a browser tab
6. Have a second tab open with a brief version of the automation flow (GHL demo account or a slide/video you've prepared)

---

## What to Show on Screen

- Start on their Google Business Profile or website
- Zoom in on their phone number and call button
- Reference their reviews briefly
- Transition to showing the automation flow

---

## Script

> **[0:00 to 0:20 - Introduction]**
>
> "Hey [First Name], this is [Your Name] from Summit Automation. I recorded this quick video specifically for [Business Name]. It'll take about two minutes, and I think you'll find it useful. Let me show you what I found."

---

> **[0:20 to 1:00 - Show Their Profile and Identify the Problem]**
>
> "So here's [Business Name] on Google. You've got [X] reviews, which is great. That tells me you're getting calls, people know you, and business is coming in.
>
> Here's the thing though: I tested what happens when someone calls your number and doesn't get an answer. I called at [time] on [day], and it went straight to voicemail.
>
> For a homeowner with a roof problem, that's often enough for them to just hit the back button and call the next company on the list. And based on what I'm seeing here, you've got solid volume. So there's a real chance you're losing calls you're already paying to generate."

---

> **[1:00 to 2:00 - Show the Solution]**
>
> "Here's what we do. When that call comes in and nobody answers, our system fires off a text within seconds. Something like this: [show example first SMS on screen].
>
> The homeowner replies, and the AI takes over. It asks about their roof, their address, whether there's an insurance claim involved, and when they want someone to come out. [Show a short example conversation if you have one.]
>
> By the time [Business Name] gets the notification, you already know the homeowner's name, their address, what's going on with the roof, and when they want to book. That's the whole thing."

---

> **[2:00 to 2:30 - Call to Action]**
>
> "I'd love to put together a full missed call audit for you specifically and show you what this would look like set up for [Business Name].
>
> It's free, takes about 15 minutes on a call, and you'll walk away knowing exactly how many calls you might be losing and what that's worth in revenue.
>
> Reply to this video or text me at [number] and we'll get something on the calendar. Thanks, [First Name], I hope this was useful."

---

## Tips for Recording

- Record in one take if possible. Authenticity beats polish.
- Keep it under 3 minutes. After 3 minutes, drop-off increases significantly.
- Use Loom's click-highlight feature to draw attention to what you're showing
- Keep your face in the corner the whole time (it builds trust)
- Do not read from a script word for word. Use this as a guide and talk naturally.
- Re-record if you stumble badly, but minor stumbles are fine and human

---

## Where to Send Loom Videos

- Email: paste the Loom link in the body (subject line: "Quick video I recorded for [Company Name]")
- Instagram DM: paste the link after the first exchange
- Text message: if you have their cell, a plain text with the link works very well
