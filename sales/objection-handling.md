# Objection Handling Guide - Summit Automation

These are the most common objections local service business owners raise and how to respond. Keep responses calm, confident, and short.

---

## "I'm too busy right now."

**What they mean:** This is not a top priority right now.

**Response:**
> "Totally understand. That is actually why a lot of business owners reach out. The busier you are, the more calls you are missing. The system runs on its own, so you do not have to manage it. Setup just takes one call on your end. When does your busy season settle down a bit? I will follow up then if now is not the time."

---

## "We don't really miss calls."

**What they mean:** They probably do, but they are not tracking it.

**Response:**
> "Fair enough. Out of curiosity, what happens when someone calls after hours or on a weekend? Or when your whole team is with a client? I ask because most businesses have gaps, and they are usually surprised how much is slipping through when we look at the actual numbers. Would a free audit be worth it just to confirm either way?"

---

## "We already have someone who answers."

**What they mean:** They think this is only for businesses with no receptionist.

**Response:**
> "That is great. The system works alongside whoever is answering. It handles overflow: after-hours calls, calls during jobs, calls when the line is busy. It also follows up automatically when someone calls and hangs up before your team gets to it. Does your current setup cover those gaps?"

---

## "I tried something like this before and it didn't work."

**What they mean:** They have been burned by a generic bot or automation tool.

**Response:**
> "I hear that a lot. Most of those tools are generic chatbots that feel robotic and frustrate the customer. What we build is tuned to how your type of business actually works: the questions the AI asks, the handoff logic, the notification the team gets. I would love to show you the actual conversation flow. That will tell you pretty quickly if this is different from what you tried."

---

## "It sounds complicated."

**What they mean:** They do not want to learn new software or manage a new tool.

**Response:**
> "Completely understandable. The good news is you do not have to touch any of it. We build it, set it up, and test it. Your job is to approve it and take follow-up calls on qualified leads. The only thing you see on your end is a notification that says 'new lead' with all the details. That is it."

---

## "We get most of our business from referrals."

**What they mean:** They do not see themselves as a high-volume inbound call business.

**Response:**
> "Referrals are great. But when someone is referred to you and they call to schedule, what happens if you miss that call? Even one referral lost because nobody answered is real money. This system makes sure every single call gets followed up on, including the referrals that are the most valuable to protect."

---

## "That's too expensive."

**What they mean:** The ROI is not clear yet.

**Response:**
> "I get it. Let me put it in perspective. Setup is $1,000, then $500 a month. If this system captures one additional booked appointment per month that you would have otherwise lost to a missed call, the setup pays for itself in the first job for most service businesses. Does that math make sense for your average customer value?"

If they push back:
> "What would need to be true for this to be a clear yes for you? I want to make sure I understand what matters most before we talk next steps."

---

## "Send me some information."

**What they mean:** Could be genuine interest or a polite brush-off.

**Response:**
> "Happy to. What format works best, a one-page summary or a short video showing how it works? I can also put together a missed call audit for your business specifically. Which would be most useful?"

Do not send a generic PDF. Send the one-pager or a Loom video. Follow up within 3 days.

---

## "I need to think about it."

**What they mean:** Interested but not ready to commit.

**Response:**
> "Makes sense. What is the main thing you are thinking through? I want to make sure I have answered everything before you make a decision."

Listen. If there is a specific concern, address it. Then give a clear next step:

> "No problem. How about I follow up with you on [specific day]? Does that work?"

---

## "I'll have my partner/manager look at it."

**What they mean:** There is another decision maker involved.

**Response:**
> "Of course. Would it be easier to loop them in on a quick call so everyone hears it at the same time and can ask questions? That usually speeds things up and avoids the back-and-forth."

---

## "We're not tech-savvy."

**What they mean:** Fear of getting stuck managing something they do not understand.

**Response:**
> "That is honestly the best client for us. We do everything. You do not need to log into any dashboard or configure anything. Once it is set up, you just get a notification on your phone when a new lead is ready. That is the entire experience on your end."
