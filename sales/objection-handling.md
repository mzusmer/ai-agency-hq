# Objection Handling Guide - Summit Automation

These are the most common objections roofing company owners raise and how to respond. Keep responses calm, confident, and short.

---

## "I'm too busy right now."

**What they mean:** This is not a top priority. I don't want to deal with new things.

**Response:**
> "Totally understand. That's actually why a lot of roofing companies reach out to us. The busier you are, the more calls you're missing. The system runs on its own, so you don't have to manage it. The setup just takes one call on your end. When does your busy season slow down a bit? I'll follow up then if now's not the time."

---

## "We don't really miss calls."

**What they mean:** They probably do, but they're not aware of it or don't want to admit it.

**Response:**
> "Fair enough. Out of curiosity, what happens when someone calls after hours or on a Sunday? Or when your whole team is on a roof? I ask because most roofing companies do have gaps, and they're usually surprised how much is slipping through when we dig into it. Would it be worth a quick free audit just to see the actual numbers for your business?"

---

## "We already have someone who answers."

**What they mean:** They think this doesn't apply to them.

**Response:**
> "That's great. Having someone on the phone is valuable. The system we build works alongside that. It handles the overflow: after-hours calls, calls during jobs, calls when the receptionist is busy. It also follows up automatically when someone calls and hangs up before your team gets to it. Does your current setup cover those scenarios?"

---

## "I tried something like this before and it didn't work."

**What they mean:** They've been burned by a generic automation tool.

**Response:**
> "I hear that a lot. Most of those systems are generic chatbots that feel robotic and annoy the homeowner. What we build is roofing-specific. The questions the AI asks are exactly what a good sales rep would ask on a first call: what's going on with the roof, is there an insurance claim, how urgent is it. The homeowner experience is completely different. I'd love to show you the actual conversation flow. That would tell you pretty quickly if this is different from what you've tried."

---

## "It sounds complicated."

**What they mean:** They don't want to learn new software.

**Response:**
> "Completely understandable. The good news is you don't have to touch any of it. We build it, we set it up, we test it. Your job is to approve it and take calls from qualified leads. The only thing you see on your end is a notification that says 'new hot lead' with all the details. That's it."

---

## "We get mostly referrals, not cold calls."

**What they mean:** They don't see themselves as a high-volume inbound lead business.

**Response:**
> "Referrals are great. But when someone is referred to you and they call to set up an estimate, what happens if you miss that call? Even one referral lost because nobody answered is a job that walked out the door. This system makes sure every single call gets followed up on, including referrals. Those are the highest-value leads to protect."

---

## "That's too expensive."

**What they mean:** The ROI is not clear yet, or they're comparing to zero (doing nothing).

**Response:**
> "I get it. Let me put it in perspective. Setup is $1,000. Most roofing companies have an average job value of somewhere between $8,000 and $15,000. If this system captures one additional job per month that you would have otherwise lost from a missed call, the setup pays for itself in the first job, and you're profit from there. Does that math make sense for your business?"

If they push back further:
> "What would it take for this to be a no-brainer for you? I want to make sure I understand what success looks like on your end before we talk next steps."

---

## "Send me some information."

**What they mean:** This could be genuine interest or a polite brush-off.

**Response:**
> "Happy to. What format works best for you, a one-page summary or a short video showing how it works? I can also put together a quick missed call audit for your business specifically. Which would be most useful?"

Do not send a generic PDF. Send the one-pager or a Loom video. Follow up within 3 days.

---

## "I need to think about it."

**What they mean:** They're interested but not ready to commit.

**Response:**
> "Makes sense. What's the main thing you're thinking through? I want to make sure I've answered everything before you make a decision."

Listen. If there's a specific concern, address it. If they just need time, give them a clear next step:

> "No problem. How about I follow up with you on [specific day]? Does that work?"

---

## "I'll have my partner/wife/manager look at it."

**What they mean:** There's another decision maker involved.

**Response:**
> "Of course. Would it be easier to just loop them in on a quick call so everyone hears it at the same time and can ask questions? That usually speeds things up and avoids the game of telephone."
